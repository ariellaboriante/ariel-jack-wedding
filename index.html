Our Story

How We Met

Our story begins in our first year of university. We met each other in physics class and bonded over busy schedules, formula sheets, and working late nights in the engineering lab. However, it wasn't until the summer of 2014 that we started talking to each other and one day we decided to meet up. We agreed to invite our friends to watch Guardians of the Galaxy at Fairview mall. To the other's surprise, we both showed up alone and said our friends were unavailable. That was our first date. Since then we've been like peas and carrots, graduating together, travelling to new and exciting places, and renovating our first home from top to bottom. However, the greatest achievement we have yet to accomplish is to start our own family. It's been our dream for quite a while and we can't wait for you to be there with us!

How He Proposed

On April 16, 2022, we went on a spontaneous Vegas road trip where we planned to hike Angel's Landing at Zion National Park. After a chaotic start of the day which involved picking up our jeep camper, learning how to set up the attached tent, buying groceries, crossing the Nevada-Utah border and finding out we "lost" an hour because of the time change, the day transition to one where everything fell right into place. Since we arrived late, everyone was already heading down the trail so when we got to the mountain summit we had the whole place to ourselves! We were at a dangerous yet scenic point in the trail and I stopped to take a video of the amazing view. When I turned around, Jack was already on one knee. He opened his wallet and produced a ring that he managed to keep from the person that packs his suitcase. We both cried at the unfolding of events that occured that day as I told Jack that incredibly, he proposed to me in my namesake place. It's no coincidence because my personalized proposal came from a person specially made for me!

Getting There

The best way to get to Banff is to fly to Calgary and then rent a car at the airport. From there, Banff is about 1 hour and 45 minutes. For budget-friendly accommodations, we recommend staying at Canmore, which is only a 30-minute drive away from Banff Gondola. Note that you will need a National Park Pass to visit Banff, Yoho and Jasper. To get the most out of your trip, we recommend purchasing the Family/Group Discovery Pass online and bringing a print copy with you.

FAQ


When is the latest I can RSVP?
Kindly RSVP no later than August 21st, 2023.

What is the dress code for the wedding?
Please dress in formal attire. Our ceremony will be outdoors so remember to bring a coat!

What will the weather be like in Banff this time of year?
Spring weather can be unpredictable in Banff. We recommend wearing layers and bringing a jacket. The mountain tops can be quite cold!

Where can I park?
Parking is available at the Banff gondola for free.

What happens after the ceremony?
Please stay after the ceremony for group photos. You'll have some time to explore the trail before cocktail hours starts. At 5 PM, please head to our wedding reception on the third floor for the Sky Bistro restaurant.

Does the wedding have a theme?
We ask everyone to wear blue, purple or pink in keeping with our theme!

What time should I arrive?
Please arrive at 1:00 pm to allow for the upload time on the cable car.

Are kids welcome?
While we love your little ones, our wedding is going to be an adults-only event so that everyone can relax and enjoy the evening. We appreciate you making arrangements ahead of time so you can celebrate with us.

Do you have a wedding registry?
We do not have a wedding registry but we would appreciate a gift towards our honeymoon!
